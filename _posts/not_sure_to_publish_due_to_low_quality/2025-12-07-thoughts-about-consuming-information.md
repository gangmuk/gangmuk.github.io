PhD is not judged by the amount of knowledge/information one has. The amount of information and the research quantity or quality is not necessarily proportional. 

Consuming information is fun and relatively easy and relatively passive. You consume knowledge created by someone else. 

The feedback loop is relatively short and human (maybe animal)'s brain is designed to be susceptible to short-term rewards. 

It is easy to spend too much time consuming information, addicted to this short-term reward, the feeling that you are doing something.

It is dangerous. It is not bad. you need to learn. and you are learning something definitely useful. 'Useful' as in technical. How GPU works, how K8s works, how TCP works, how NIC works, how vLLM scheduler works, etc.
