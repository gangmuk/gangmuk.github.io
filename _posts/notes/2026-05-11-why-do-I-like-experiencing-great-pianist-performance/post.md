Why do you think people go to classical concerts or concerts in general? I don’t think people go just to listen to good music.

I realized this after experiencing a Lang Lang Bach recital. It was my very first time going to a recital—he played the Goldberg Variations at the Krannert Center in Champaign, Illinois. It was deeply impressive and the more I think about it afterwards, the more inspiring it becomes. The full experience has been happening even after the performance ended by thinking about it. The amount of effort and repetition that he put in to make it as perfect as possible would be a lot, including perseverance, contemplation, practice, focus.

In performances, people can feel it indirectly. I think people are inspired to see that and this inspiring feeling is what human wants from the performances. The piano sound and good music is not the root reason of people going to the performances. It is just a result of their practice and their emotions on the song. It is a medium to feel those works that the artist put in. By experiencing the performance, what you actually see is their life. Maybe the word "life" sounds like a bit of a stretch, but people feel so pleased to witness how great thing a human can achieve through the performance as manifestation.

Yes, of course, the music is good and the sound is pleasing as well, but that’s just one little piece of everything. The more essential thing is that you indirectly experience the effort, the time, and the passion that the performer put in. You indirectly feel it, and that feeling is deeply pleasing. People (at least I) felt the greatness of the performer's work and see the process over the wall. And that's why people are eager to experience from watching the performances.

And I see myself and me with my work. It made me think that I also want to be great. I want to be also great man. I don't know why but being great looks beautiful and think that it might fulfill the life possibly. Maybe it is one of the key in life.

If that performer has such great passion, personality, and talent, and if they are experiencing the absolute fullness of whatever they are doing—whether it is playing the piano, singing a song, dancing, or playing the electric guitar—people want to observe how great a human being can be. And we are also the human, so we can do that too. Whatever the form is, you aren't just there for the sound. You are there for the feeling of indirectly being that great person in that moment and as a potential great version of yourself.
